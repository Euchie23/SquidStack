# 🐙 SquidStack – Dietary Risk Evaluation for Cephalopods

## Context & Problem Framing
Seafood, particularly cephalopods like *Illex argentinus*, can bioaccumulate heavy metals and organic contaminants, transferring directly into human diets. 

This assessment focuses on two countries: **Argentina**, where the squid were primarily caught in the Southwest Atlantic Ocean, and **Taiwan**, where squid consumption is relatively high. These countries were selected to demonstrate the tool’s applicability across different consumption patterns and population exposures.  

Public health authorities and policymakers face a critical challenge: **they need to know whether contaminant measurements are reliable enough to inform human health risk assessment and guide sustainable seafood consumption recommendations**.  

The **Risk Evaluation Dashboard** within SquidStack addresses the key decision question:

> **How can these measurements be used to guide sustainable seafood consumption, regulatory priorities, and targeted monitoring of high-risk contaminants?**

By integrating analytical reliability, exposure scenarios, and toxicological thresholds, this tool translates complex environmental data into actionable decision insights.

## Analytical Approach & Methodology
- **Validated metrics:** Metals were measured and cross-checked with certified reference materials (CRMs), ensuring credible data for decision support.  
- **Limitations:** Certain metals (e.g., Metal_I) could only be evaluated relatively due to CRM gaps. Organics were analyzed with adapted methods; full recovery validation is ongoing.  
- **Scenarios & assumptions:** Two countries (Argentina and Taiwan) evaluated under Normal and Extreme (95th percentile) consumption scenarios.  
- **Risk metrics:** Hazard Quotients (HQ) and exposure calculations were used to identify priority contaminants.  

---

## Key Findings

### Metals
- **Persistent risk:** Metals I (highly toxic), D (essential), and A (essential) consistently exceeded or approached health-based guidance values, signaling **priority risk**.  
- **Scenario impact:** Taiwan’s higher consumption scenarios (Normal & Extreme) showed elevated HQs, suggesting **emerging risk and monitoring necessity**.  
- **Temporal trends:** Peaks in May 2019, April 2020, and June 2019–2021 indicate **specific windows of concern**, useful for planning targeted monitoring campaigns.  

### Organics
- Generally low risk; only Organic_C occasionally exceeded screening thresholds.  
- Trends reinforce the **low likelihood of dietary health concern** under current exposure assumptions.  

### Supporting Context: Fluctuations Dashboard
While the Risk Evaluation app translates contaminant data into dietary exposure and HQs, the Fluctuations Dashboard contextualizes **historical pollutant trends** and bioaccumulation across tissues and years. This enables decision-makers to:  
- Identify periods of elevated risk or temporary reprieve (e.g., COVID-19 lockdowns).  
- Prioritize signals for further investigation or early-stage monitoring.  

The dashboard **supports the Risk Evaluation module** by showing when environmental pressures may amplify or dampen contaminant exposure, strengthening **temporal situational awareness**.  

---

## Lessons Learned / Consultancy Insights
1. **Decision-focused presentation is key:** Presenting hazard metrics alongside consumption scenarios makes data actionable, not just descriptive.  
2. **Transparency builds trust:** Explicitly stating limitations (e.g., CRM gaps, species-specific assumptions) allows stakeholders to make **informed risk judgments**.  
3. **Scenario analysis informs resource allocation:** Comparing Normal vs Extreme consumption highlights where regulators should focus monitoring, public advisories, or regulatory action.  
4. **Integration with historical trends enhances context:** Dashboards like Fluctuations provide **strategic foresight** for potential environmental pressures.  
5. **Portfolio value:** Demonstrates ability to combine **data science, environmental chemistry, and decision-support design** into consultancy-ready deliverables.

## Impact / Takeaways for Decision-Makers
- Provides **screening-level risk evaluation** for human dietary exposure from squid consumption.  
- Highlights **priority metals and exposure windows** for targeted monitoring and regulatory focus.  
- Offers a **transparent, reproducible, scenario-driven framework** supporting early-warning decisions.  
- Delivers **actionable insights** for public health authorities, fisheries managers, and environmental regulators while communicating limits of the underlying data.
